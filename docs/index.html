Hi there! My name is Maryn Sanders (she/her), and I'm a Ph.D. candidate working with Josh Roering at the University of Oregon. I am currently studying how climate change is changing the frequency and magnitude of shallow landslides and debris flows in two regions: (1) Western Oregon, where fires are becoming more intense and frequent, and (2) Southeast Alaska, where rain events are becoming more intense and frequent.

I received my undergraduate degree in Geophysics from the University of California, Berkeley, where my passion for hillslope geomorphology initiated during my undergraduate research project with Bill Dietrich on rainfall-induced shallow landslide failure thresholds at a field site in the Northern California Coast Range.

After graduating, I worked as a research assistant for the Eel River Critical Zone Observatory, where I collected data on vadose zone groundwater dynamics and soil moisture stable isotopes.
